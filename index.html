VEX Claw Assembly & Documentation

Engineering Drawing

This project demonstrates **detail orientation and organization** by creating a full assembly drawing with a complete Bill of Materials (BOM) for a VEX Robotics claw. Mastery of complex assembly constraints and standardized documentation is essential for manufacturing, showcasing readiness for BIM/VDC and Project Management roles.

The assembly involved over 15 unique VEX parts, including custom-designed claw fingers and linkages. The drawing follows ANSI standards and includes exploded views, part ballooning, and a detailed BOM specifying quantities and part numbers, directly supporting procurement and assembly processes. This rigorous approach is a core skill for BIM/VDC roles.

Precision Machined Arbor Press

Mechanical Design

A complex 3D modeling exercise focused on simulating a functional arbor press. This project required high **resolve and consistency** in part design, surfacing, and maintaining geometric tolerances, reflecting the standards required in Manufacturing and Field Engineering.

Every component of the press, from the base and ram to the geared ratchet, was modeled individually with precise dimensions and mating features. The final assembly used advanced mates (like gear mates and slider joints) to simulate the press's full range of motion. This deep dive into complex assemblies demonstrates technical "Grit" and problem-solving skills necessary for Engineering roles.

VEX Component Latch Mechanism

Assembly Prototyping

A practical **problem-solving** challenge using standard VEX components to design a robust latching mechanism. This demonstrates a strong **purpose** in applying CAD skills to real-world mechanical constraints and assembly practices, which is crucial for on-site execution and Field roles.

The primary goal was creating a secure, quick-release locking system entirely from standard VEX components (shaft, beams, fasteners). The design was iterated three times to minimize stress points and maximize locking strength, ultimately achieving a simple, elegant solution. This iterative process highlights adaptability and customer-focused design.
